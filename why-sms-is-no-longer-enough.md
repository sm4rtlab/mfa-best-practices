# Why SMS-Based Authentication Is No Longer Enough

SMS verification was once considered a major improvement over password-only authentication.

Today, threat actors increasingly target SMS-based systems through:

- SIM swapping
- Social engineering
- Mobile malware
- Intercepted messages

## Common Weaknesses

### SIM Swapping

Attackers convince mobile carriers to transfer a victim's phone number to another device.

### Device Compromise

Malware on mobile devices can access SMS messages.

### Delayed Delivery

SMS codes may be delayed or not delivered at all.

## Better Alternatives

Organizations increasingly deploy:

- Authenticator applications
- FIDO2 security keys
- Passkeys
- Hardware authentication tokens

## FIDO2 Advantages

FIDO2 provides:

- Phishing-resistant authentication
- Strong cryptographic protection
- Improved user experience
- Reduced account takeover risks

## Conclusion

SMS authentication can still provide additional protection compared to passwords alone, but it should not be considered the strongest available MFA option.
